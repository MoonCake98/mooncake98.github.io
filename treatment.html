SCID

Treatment of SCID

There are several treament options for SCID like stemcell therapy, Gene Therapy and Enzyme Replacement Therapy. Subtypes of SCID can require different treament plans, this will be explained further below.

Overview: The treatment options:

  • Stemcell transplantation (HSCT)
  • Enzyme Replacement Therapy
  • Gene Therapy

Stemcell transplantation (HSCT)

Hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT) can be used to treat SCID but since SCID does not have the same root cause in all patients, so it's a "heterogeneous disease". This means that the succes rate of replacing The patients stem cells will not always have the same outcome.

In 1968, the first transplant of allogeneic bone marrow was given. And for the first decade after, an HLA identical donor was required to avoid the lethal graft vs host disease. After that, T cell depleted transplants have been possible from HLA haploidentical related donors since 1981. and even more recent from match unrelated donors. 4
Bonemarrow transplantation has resulted in lifesaving T-cell reconstitution in infants with SCID but the correction of B-cell function has been more problematic.

Enzyme Replacement Therapy

In some cases, SCID is caused by adenosine deaminase deficiency. (2) Adenosine deaminase is an enzyme which catalyzes the irreversible deamination of adenosine and deoxyadenosine to insosine and deoxyinosine. This enzyme occures everywhere in the body.

Gene Therapy

ADA-SCID is the first disease treated by gene-therapy.
