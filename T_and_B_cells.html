SCID

The effect of SCID on T and B cells

The carecterization of diffrent SCID types and their effect on the imuunm system.

SCID T and B cells

SCID is carecterized as a T-B cell deficiencie wich leads to a weakend imuunm system. The type of SCID is dependend on what gene is mutated and wich cells suffer under that effect. In the text below are some examples. The (T/B/NK) stands for T-cells, B-Cells and Natural Killer Cells. The + means the Cells are present, and - means the cells arent present regardless of function

X-linked SCID

Gene: IL2R T-/B+/NK- The Interluekin-2 is a T-cell based cytokine that is responsible for sending signals to other cells that can turn off their growth, diffrentation and function. The Receptor Gamma chain of this gene together with the Beta chain is responsible for the binding affinity and intercellular transduction. If there are mutations in the gamma chain it leads to a near if not complete loss of function of the T-cells.[1] Even tho there are B cells present in normal volumes in a patient with X-linked SCID these cells do not function.[2]

RAG-1 and RAG-2 Deficiency SCID

Gene: RAG-1 and RAG-2 T-/B-/NK+ The RAG 1 and 2 genes code for a endonuclease protein that is responsible for recombining the VDJ segments of a lymphocyte cell. If there are mutations in this gene it results in T-/B-cells that are unable to form receptors leading to a complete loss of function of these 2 celltypes. It is posible for milder mutations of this gene to still make partialy functioning proteins wich can lead to the formation to some T-/B-Cell formation. This is usualy carecterized as "leaky" SCID where there is still a case of an auto-imuunm diseasy but in a milder form.[3]
