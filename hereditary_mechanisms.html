The hereditary nature of SCID

Autosomal Recessive Inheritance

Most forms of SCID are autosomal recessive, both parents are the carriers of one mutated gene but do not have symptoms themselves. The child has 25% change of inheriting two mutated genes (and developing SCID), 50% chance of being only a carrier like their parents and a 25% chance of getting two normal, not mutated genes. [1]

Paternal and Maternal Inheritance

In the case of X-based SCID the recessive gene is guaranteed to pass on to the daughter from the paternal line, But cant pass the gene on to the son. The maternal line will always have the chance to pass on the affected gene to their child regardless of gender[1]
